What Counts as Travel
  • A travel tournament is any tournament 90+ miles from the Valor office at 4455 Brookfield Corporate Drive, Chantilly, VA 20151, and requiring a hotel stay. Travel is defined by distance, not driving time.
  • Local tournaments do not qualify.
  • Same-day trips 90+ miles away with no hotel (State Cup, Presidents Cup) can be reimbursed up to $50 gas and $35 food.
  • For ECNL-RL and Pre-ECNL league games more than 90 miles out with a kickoff at 10:00 am or earlier, one night's hotel the night before is eligible.
Submitting Your Request
  • You must provide an itemized receipt within 7 days of your completed event. Without an itemized receipt we cannot reimburse you — no exceptions.
  • All receipts must arrive as one single PDF. This tool does that for you automatically.
  • Submit everything with your initial request. No supplemental requests are accepted.
  • Reimbursements come separately from your payroll deposit. They are not included with your pay because that would make the amount taxable income, which it is not.
Hotel
  • Stay at the same hotel as your team and use the same booking company, to avoid no-show fees if the tournament is canceled.
  • You can book through your manager's team link, or your manager can reserve for you — if they do, make sure your card is on file at check-in.
  • Standard room class only. In-room and personal charges are not reimbursable.
  • An itemized hotel folio is required.
Meals
  • Up to $45 per day, including tax and tip.
  • Two-day tournament: $90 max. Three-day showcase: $135 max.
  • Alcohol is not reimbursed, but you still need the itemized receipt even if alcohol appears on it — that amount is deducted when your total is calculated.
  • No meals eaten locally before you leave or after you return.
Personal Vehicle & Gas
  • Use your personal vehicle for out-of-town tournaments where appropriate.
  • 90+ miles: gas up to $100 for the whole weekend. 60–90 miles: up to $50.
  • You are responsible for carrying adequate insurance. Never transport players in your personal vehicle.
  • Valor is not responsible for damage to your vehicle on these trips.
  • On team bus trips, coaches travel with the team to reduce expense.
Car Rentals
  • If the team flew, a rental car may be reimbursed. Choose mid-size, or full-size if transporting more than two people. Never transport players in a rental.
  • Rent only when cheaper than rideshare, taxi, or airport shuttle. Carpool with families and other coaches wherever possible.
  • Accept the collision insurance offered by the agency; decline all others.
  • For multi-van trips (Phoenix, Disney) the coach should be listed as a driver, but drivers should be parents or the team manager, with collision and liability coverage.
  • Decline the prepaid gas option. Gas for rentals is reimbursable with documentation.
  • After an accident, contact the rental company, your insurer, local authorities as required, the Director of Coaching, and the team manager. Photograph damage to both vehicles.
Air & Rail Travel
  • Book air travel with your team.
  • Secure the lowest available fare, book more than 45 days ahead, and check both Dulles and Reagan National. Use non-direct flights when the savings are substantial ($200+). Work with Beth Morris to book.
  • Economy class only. Book roundtrip, not two one-ways.
  • Changes made for personal reasons are on you. Pre-boarding, inflight food, and Wi-Fi are not reimbursable.
  • Airport and train station parking must be long-term lots — reimbursable with receipt.
  • If a trip is canceled after ticketing, work with Beth to reuse the ticket or minimize cost to the club.
Not Reimbursable
  • Alcohol, entertainment, loss of personal items, travel for additional family members, and deductible payments for vehicle damage.
  • Tolls (changed for 2026–27).

Submitting a request confirms you have read and understand these policies, and that Valor will not reimburse expenses that do not comply with them. Questions go to Beth Morris.
